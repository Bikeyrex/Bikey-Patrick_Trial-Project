Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist and humanitarian who led initiatives worldwide that contributed to the extensive increases in agricultural production termed the Green Revolution.

He was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970 for his contributions to world peace through increasing food supply.

Borlaug is often credited with saving millions of lives through his work, which helped to alleviate hunger and starvation in many countries around the world.
